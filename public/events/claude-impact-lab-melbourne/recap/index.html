9:00 AM · The day opens

A few words from Anthropic.

Martine Nilsen, from the Claude Code community team at Anthropic, joined remotely to welcome the room and frame the day.

Martine welcomed the room with a milestone worth marking: this was the first Claude Impact Lab in the Southern Hemisphere.

A guide from Rye & Gary

Why Impact Labs matter.

Rye framed the Impact Lab model: build genuinely useful tools on real public data, for the community. Gary welcomed the room to Melbourne and set the spirit for the day.

The modelOne-day, community-run, sponsored by Anthropic through the Ambassadors Program. Impact over prizes.
The momentumSan Diego, Mexico City, Miami, Santiago, and now APAC.
The spiritCollaborative, curious and pointed at the city. Make something real, and have a good Saturday doing it.

Three civic-data tracks on the board. Teams chose the problem that pulled at them, then turned a dataset into something a Melburnian could actually use.

Teams locked · clock started

Time to go for it.

Three civic-data tracks on the board. Teams voted, locked a direction, and got building. The room chose community & citizen initiatives: how might residents and communities drive their own solutions — from circular economy models to local resource sharing — that build resilience from the ground up?

The day

Six sprints, heads down.

Teams worked through six sprint blocks across the day, focused on talking and building, then submitted their ideas and pitched them at 5pm.

The outcomes

What teams built.

By late afternoon, every team took the stage for a strict, timed pitch in front of a judging panel that included the Lord Mayor's office. Eight teams, eight civic problems — here's what they built.

★ Demo day

“Melbourne has a waste problem.”

Each team brought its own local angle, and didn't just pitch an idea. Every one demoed a working prototype, built on live data.

8 teams pitched Judged on real-world impact
Safety & mobility

CityStride

Live “comfort” walking routes that score every Melbourne street by how alive it feels right now — pedestrian sensors, lighting, tree canopy and venue density — then route you by Lively, Accessible or Shortest.

8 builders · Live demo ↗
Circular economy

Ecoblock

Tackling Melbourne's hard-waste problem with a circular-economy approach — keeping usable material out of landfill and back into the community.

10 builders · GitHub ↗
Belonging

spacd

A map-based platform that helps international students and new residents beat isolation — surfacing low-pressure community events at public BBQs, gardens and shared kitchens, with AI onboarding and RSVPs.

7 builders · Live demo ↗
Waste & reuse

The Loop

A circular-resource router that intercepts the moment of disposal and finds the nearest place to reuse, repair or recycle an item — ranked by impact, with an honest impact receipt and a Claude Desktop MCP server.

7 builders · GitHub ↗
🏆 Winner

Good Sh*t

The day's winning build — a map that makes local businesses' civic and sustainability work visible. Shops publish their good deeds and earn “Heart Points” while Melburnians browse, applaud and follow a public leaderboard.

5 builders · GitHub ↗
Newcomers

Tapestry

An AI concierge that helps relocating families settle and belong — building personalised practical and cultural transition plans, and routing newcomers into existing community groups.

7 builders · GitHub ↗
Wellbeing

Neighbourhood Pulse

A voice-enabled check-in companion that helps isolated seniors find nearby support, paired with a privacy-safe council dashboard that surfaces community-connection gaps across suburbs.

7 builders · GitHub ↗
Green space

Finding Common Ground

Helps neighbours turn forgotten urban green spaces into community gardens — checking site feasibility on sunlight, soil and water, then auto-generating permit applications and signage.

9 builders · Pitch deck ↗
